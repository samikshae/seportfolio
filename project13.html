Truss Design - Structural Analysis & Testing Project

2022

This project focused on designing, analyzing, and physically testing truss structures constructed from popsicle sticks and wood glue. Each truss was built to specific dimensional constraints and tested under load to evaluate strength, efficiency, and failure behavior.

Through analytical calculations and experimental testing, I learned how to determine internal and external forces within truss members and identify potential failure points. I applied free-body diagrams, assessed statistical determinacy, and used MDSolids to generate truss resolution diagrams. Observing real-world failures allowed me to compare theoretical predictions with physical results, reinforcing the relationship between calculations and material behavior.

The testing process provided valuable insight into how compression, tension, joint integrity, and construction quality affect structural performance. Post-test analysis informed potential design improvements, such as increasing joint stiffness, improving material strength, and optimizing member placement using triangular configurations to enhance load distribution and overall efficiency.
