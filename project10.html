Compound Machine Design - Mechanical Advantage Project

2023

This project focused on designing a compound machine capable of lifting a 500 g mass vertically by 3 inches while achieving a total mechanical advantage between 5 and 15. The system was required to integrate multiple simple machines into a single functional mechanism.

The final design incorporated a pulley system, chain and sprocket, wheel and axle, and a compound gear train assembled using VEX components. Mechanical advantage calculations were performed for each subsystem and combined to determine the total system performance, resulting in a calculated mechanical advantage of 14.968. An isometric sketch was also produced to document the design layout.

Through this project, I developed a strong understanding of ideal mechanical advantage calculations for both simple and compound machines. The experience emphasized the importance of planning, intermediate checkpoints, and verification of calculations, as early errors led to additional redesign time and reinforced the value of systematic problem-solving.
