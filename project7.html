Composite Materials Fabrication & Testing — Materials Engineering

2023

This project focused on fabricating and testing fiberglass composite samples to evaluate their mechanical properties. I investigated how sample dimensions influence tensile strength and modulus of elasticity, observing that larger cross-sectional dimensions allowed higher maximum loads prior to failure.

The fabrication process highlighted the sensitivity of composite performance to manufacturing quality. A primary challenge was minimizing air bubbles in the epoxy-resin matrix, as voids negatively affect structural integrity. Careful resin application and surface smoothing were required to produce testable samples.

The project reinforced the importance of material volume control during fabrication. Using less resin earlier in the process would have reduced air entrapment and improved sample consistency, leading to more efficient manufacturing and improved test results.
