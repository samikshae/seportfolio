Airfoil Simulation — Aerodynamics & Data Analysis

2024

This project examined the aerodynamic behavior of airfoil shapes under varying angles of attack, with a focus on an elliptical airfoil profile. Simulation data showed that increasing angle of attack raised the coefficient of lift up to an optimal point, after which lift decreased due to aerodynamic limitations.

The project improved my ability to analyze aerodynamic data and interpret lift and drag relationships. Initial assumptions of an inverse relationship between lift and drag were corrected through analysis of simulation graphs, demonstrating that both coefficients can increase simultaneously depending on flow conditions.

This experience strengthened my data interpretation skills and reinforced the importance of understanding aerodynamic trade-offs rather than relying on simplified assumptions when evaluating airfoil performance.
