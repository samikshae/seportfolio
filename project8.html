Rocket Project - Propulsion & Flight Analysis

2023

This project involved designing, simulating, and launching a model rocket while analyzing its flight performance. I developed an understanding of rocket components, solid rocket engine operation, and the use of OpenRocket software to simulate flight behavior and reduce launch risk. Trigonometric analysis was applied to estimate peak altitude based on launch angle and measured ground distance.

The project emphasized hands-on troubleshooting and iterative testing during launch preparation. After multiple unsuccessful ignition attempts, systematic trial-and-error testing revealed faulty wiring as the root cause. Replacing the damaged wires resolved the issue and enabled a successful launch, reinforcing the importance of diagnostics and component reliability in aerospace systems.

Through this experience, I learned the value of early fault identification and root-cause analysis. A more detailed inspection of electrical connections at the outset would have reduced setup time and improved overall efficiency.
