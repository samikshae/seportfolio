Structural Design- Bridge Optimization

2022

This project involved designing and optimizing a functional bridge using the West Point Bridge Designer software while meeting realistic constraints related to cost, strength, and durability. The objective was to produce a design that could withstand applied loads without exceeding the given budget.

The design process began with developing and testing multiple conceptual bridge designs to evaluate structural performance and cost efficiency. Features from the strongest designs were combined into a refined model, which was iteratively tested and adjusted through trial and error. This approach allowed for informed design decisions based on performance data rather than assumptions.

Through this activity, I gained insight into the complexity of structural design and load distribution. I developed a stronger understanding of member sizing, material selection, and when to use solid versus hollow members to balance strength and cost. The project also highlighted the importance of exploring unconventional designs; while my final bridge was effective, pushing beyond standard truss patterns could have led to a more innovative and cost-efficient solution.
