Renewable Insulation - Materials Selection Project

2022

This project involved designing an environmentally friendly and cost-effective insulation material using renewable and recyclable components. The goal was to minimize heat loss while adhering to sustainability and material availability constraints.

My team evaluated several candidate materials before selecting natural cotton batting and packing tape based on accessibility and insulating potential. We fabricated an 8 in. × 8 in. insulation panel and tested its performance by placing it over a controlled heat source. Temperature measurements inside and outside the system were recorded to assess thermal resistance and overall effectiveness.

Through this project, I strengthened my understanding of thermodynamic principles and heat transfer calculations, including unit consistency and energy flow analysis. The project also improved my ability to evaluate materials based on both performance and sustainability, reinforcing the role of engineering decisions in environmental impact.
